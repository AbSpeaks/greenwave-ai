Physics Engine Architecture

GreenWave is not a routing app. It is a cloud-native computational physics engine that applies fluid mechanics, thermodynamic network theory, and kinetic calculus to dissolve urban congestion at the mathematical layer.

PHYSICS_ENG::01

Fluid Dynamics & Shockwave Dissolution

We model bumper-to-bumper traffic not as discrete vehicles but as compressible fluid flows propagating through urban conduits. The engine continuously calculates Lighthill-Whitham-Richards (LWR) wave equations to detect and dissolve traffic shockwaves—the invisible compression waves causing sudden braking—kilometres before they physically materialize.

PHYSICS_ENG::02

Kinetic Energy Conservation

Every time a 40-tonne freight vehicle stops at a red signal, it wastes enormous kinetic energy reserves, amplifying city emissions and accelerating pavement wear. Our optimizer runs continuous momentum-preservation math across heavy freight vectors, calculating minimum-loss green windows to keep commercial corridors flowing at thermodynamically optimal velocities.

PHYSICS_ENG::03

Macroscopic Fundamental Diagrams (MFD)

We apply thermodynamic network modeling at the district level to continuously identify the exact "tipping point" where a neighborhood transitions from fluid flow to absolute gridlock saturation. By monitoring MFD state-space coordinates in real-time, the engine dynamically adjusts macro-boundary control pressures to prevent critical density from being reached.

Deployment Model

100% Pure Software Cloud Engine

GreenWave operates exclusively through cloud-to-cloud API connections and cloud-edge software overrides. The engine ingests purely digital GPS telemetry vectors and city network data streams via encrypted API channels—requiring no physical infrastructure, no embedded devices, and no hardware procurement of any kind. The entire operational stack runs in software.
